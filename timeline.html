Generation by Generation

Four distinct chapters, each one building on the last. Here is the full story of every Audi S3 ever produced — the engineering, the numbers, and what made each generation matter.

First Generation

1999 – 2003

Audi S3 8L

The car that invented a category.

Engine

1.8T 20v

Power

210 hp

Torque

270 Nm

0–60 mph

6.6 sec

Top Speed

152 mph

Gearbox

6-spd Manual

  • First compact hot hatch with standard quattro all-wheel drive via Haldex coupling
  • 1.8-litre 20-valve turbocharged engine producing 270 Nm from just 1,950 rpm
  • Sport-tuned suspension and Brembo front brake calipers fitted as standard
  • Enhanced 225 hp state of tune available from 2001 in select markets
  • Three-door bodywork only — no Sportback or saloon in this generation
  • Nogaro Blue Pearl Effect became the defining colour of an era
  • Platform: PQ34, shared with the A3 8L — kerb weight approx. 1,395 kg
Second Generation

2006 – 2012

Audi S3 8P

More power, more body styles, more technology.

Engine

2.0 TFSI

Power

265 hp

Torque

350 Nm

0–60 mph

5.6 sec

Top Speed

155 mph

Gearbox

Manual / S tronic

  • All-new 2.0 TFSI engine replaces the 1.8T, delivering 350 Nm of torque
  • S tronic 6-speed dual-clutch gearbox offered for the first time in the S3
  • Optional Magnetic Ride adaptive damping — a segment debut
  • Haldex 4th generation quattro system with faster clutch response
  • Launched as 3-door; Sportback five-door variant added in 2008
  • 2008 facelift lifted power to 268 hp and refreshed the front fascia
  • Platform: PQ35 — kerb weight approx. 1,440 kg
Third Generation

2013 – 2020

Audi S3 8V

The S3 grows up — in every direction.

Engine

2.0 TFSI EA888

Power

296 – 306 hp

Torque

380 Nm

0–60 mph

4.8 sec

Top Speed

155 mph

Gearbox

S tronic 6 / 7

  • Third-generation EA888 TFSI engine with higher compression and revised induction
  • New MQB platform brings a stiffer body, lower weight, and better dynamics
  • Audi Virtual Cockpit 12.3-inch digital instrument cluster introduced
  • Three body styles for the first time: Sportback, four-door Saloon, and Cabriolet
  • Electronic rear differential simulates torque vectoring for sharper cornering
  • 2016 facelift raises output to 306 hp with revised engine mapping and updated exterior
  • Platform: MQB — kerb weight from 1,385 kg (Sportback)
Fourth Generation

2020 – Present

Audi S3 8Y

The sharpest, most complete S3 ever built.

Power

306 hp

400 Nm

0–60 mph

4.7 sec

Gearbox

  • Fourth-generation EA888 engine paired with a 12V mild-hybrid (MHEV) system
  • MQB Evo platform — 50% more torsional stiffness than the outgoing MQB
  • True torque-vectoring rear differential: variable torque split across the rear axle
  • Audi Digital Cockpit Pro with 12.3-inch display and MMI Navigation Plus touchscreen
  • Sportback and Saloon body styles; Cabriolet discontinued for this generation
  • Drive Select with Comfort, Auto, Dynamic, and Individual modes altering all chassis systems
  • Platform: MQB Evo — kerb weight approx. 1,460 kg (Sportback)

The Architecture Beneath

Four Platforms. One Philosophy.

From the PQ34 of 1999 to the MQB Evo of today, every S3 has been engineered to the same principle: take the best platform available, make it stiffer, lower it, and fill it with as much turbocharged quattro capability as it can hold.
