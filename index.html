The Beginning

In 1999, Audi took the already-capable A3 platform, bolted in a 1.8-litre turbocharged engine, fitted quattro all-wheel drive as standard, and quietly created one of the most compelling hot hatches the world had ever seen. It wasn't the loudest car at the party — but it was almost certainly the most capable.

The formula was straightforward and devastatingly effective: a compact body, a punchy turbocharged engine, and Audi's legendary quattro all-wheel-drive system giving traction that rivals with rear- or front-wheel drive simply couldn't match. Twenty-five years on, those three ingredients remain at the heart of every S3 ever built.

From the raw, rev-hungry 1.8T of the original 8L to the torque-vectoring, mild-hybrid 2.0 TFSI of the current 8Y, the S3 has grown more powerful, more sophisticated, and more complete with every generation — without ever losing the thread that made it special in the first place.

“Not the fastest. Not the cheapest. Simply the most complete.”

Key Milestones

Moments That Defined the S3

1999

Frankfurt debut

The 8L S3 is unveiled at the IAA with 210 hp, Haldex quattro, and a 6.6-second 0–60. A new benchmark is set.

2006

2.0 TFSI era begins

The 8P replaces the beloved 1.8T with a 265 hp 2.0 TFSI, cutting 0–60 to 5.6 seconds and introducing DSG to the range.

2008

Sportback joins the range

Audi adds the five-door Sportback body to the 8P S3 lineup, bringing practicality without sacrificing any of the performance.

2013

MQB platform & Saloon

The 8V generation arrives on Audi's new MQB platform with 296 hp and — for the first time — a proper four-door Saloon body style.

2014

S3 Cabriolet arrives

An open-top S3 joins the range — four seats, full quattro, and a folding roof. The most versatile S3 bodyline ever offered.

2020

Torque vectoring & mHEV

The 8Y S3 introduces a torque-vectoring rear differential and a 12V mild-hybrid system — the most dynamically advanced S3 in history.
